## Cover note

This paper takes a deliberately narrow approach to a long-standing gap in entropic-gravity programs: the irreversible thermodynamic cost of resisting an entropic force when the energy supply has finite temperature. We do not propose a new theory of gravity. We ask a single question — *given Verlinde's screen entropy and standard non-equilibrium thermodynamics applied to a two-temperature process, what efficiency bound results, and where can it be tested?* — and identify cold-atom analog gravity as the regime where the resulting bound becomes empirically discriminating.

The paper's defensive posture is explicit throughout: a single load-bearing thermodynamic assumption is named (Assumption A, §2.3), tested numerically across five dimensions in §3, and tied to a falsification protocol in §6. Limitations are enumerated honestly in §7.

We submit this work as appropriate for CQG's analog-gravity readership and welcome adversarial review of both the construction and its proposed laboratory test.
